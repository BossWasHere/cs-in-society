AI In The Art: Jewelry

How advances in several promenent fields in computing are changing the ways we create and wear our jewels.

October 29, 2021 by Thomas Stephenson

Those with a small fortune to spare have always had the option to purchase only the most expensive, meticulously designed jewelry, encrusted by diamonds and plated by gold. For many people, jewelry signifies something important; be it marriage, the birth of a child or anything personal to the wearer. But we can get more personal than this.

Digital tools now give people the freedom to make their own designs and use their own patterns for their jewelry. Tanmay Shah, head of innovation at a company called Imaginarium in Mumbai, India said “Not just that, but the piece could be embedded with an NFT tag, which, when read by a smartphone, could trigger memories and digital memorabilia of the family, almost like a time capsule.”

How did this become possible?

Over the last few decades, improvements to Computer Aided Design software (and the power of the machines they run on) means that designers can get realtime renders of their designs and make as many changes to their jewels as they want. A lot of hardware involved in CAM (Computer Aided Manufacturing) allows for the most complex of designs to be replicated perfectly in real life. Techniques such as metal 3D printing, a novel idea to most people, have been heavily researched and improved upon, yielding high quality results particuarly when it is needed in the jewelry business.
